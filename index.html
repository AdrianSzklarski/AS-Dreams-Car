Porsche 356

Year 1960

The 356 is the first car of the Porsche brand. However, before the war the Porsche construction bureau created the Porsche 114 project with a centrally located water-cooled V-10 engine, which it planned to put into series production for the first time under the Porsche brand. The idea was torpedoed by the authorities of the Third Reich - so the project was finally adapted to the components of the series VW Garbus. This is how a model with an aerodynamic body made of aluminium - known as the Porsche Type 64 - was created in 3 copies. On the basis of this project, the Porsche Type 356 was built after World War II.

Porsche 911 993

Year 1997

The Porsche 993 is the internal designation for the Porsche 911 model manufactured and sold between January 1994 and early 1998 (model years 1995–1998 in the United States), replacing the 964. Its discontinuation marked the end of air-cooled 911 models. The 993 was much improved over and quite different from its predecessor. According to Porsche, every part of the car was designed from the ground up, including the engine and only 20% of its parts were carried over from the previous generation. Porsche refers to the 993 as "a significant advance, not just from a technical, but also a visual perspective." Porsche's engineers devised a new light-alloy subframe with coil and wishbone suspension (an all new multi-link system, Weissach axle), putting behind the previous lift-off oversteer and making significant progress with the engine and handling, creating a more civilized car overall and providing an improved driving experience. The 993 was also the first 911 to receive a six speed transmission. The 993 had several variants, as its predecessors, varying in body style, engines, drivetrains, and included equipment. Power was increased by the addition of the VarioRam system, particularly in the midranges, and also resulted in more throttle noise at higher revolutions; as a consequence, it resulted in a 15% increase in power over its predecessor. The external design of the Porsche 993, penned by English designer Tony Hatter, retained the basic body shell architecture of the 964 and other earlier 911 models, but with revised exterior panels, with much more flared wheel arches, a smoother front and rear bumper design, an enlarged retractable rear wing, and teardrop mirrors. A 993 GT2 was used as the safety car during the 1995 Formula One season.

Porsche 981

Year 2020

The factory-code 981 version made its debut at the 2012 Geneva Motor Show. The basic version has a 2.8-litre six-cylinder engine with 265 hp, its maximum torque is 280 Nm. It is centrally arranged. With a weight of 1310 kg, the car accelerates to 100 km/h in 5.8 seconds [5.5 with the PDK dual-clutch transmission]. Top speed is 264 km/h [262 km/h PDK]. The Boxster S is an upgraded version. In this version, the engine has 315 hp. The car reaches 100 km/h in 5.1 seconds [4.8 seconds PDK] and accelerates to 280 km/h. In 2013, the car together with the Cayman model won the title of World Performance Car of the Year. In March 2014, Porsche unveiled a GTS (Gran Turismo Sport) version of the Boxster. The car is powered by a Boxer engine with 330 hp and a maximum torque of 370 Nm. The car in the version with a dual-clutch PDK transmission accelerates to 100 km/h in 4.7 seconds. The car hits showrooms in May. Prices in Germany start at €69,949. In September 2014, Porsche unveiled the weakest version of the Boxster model. It is powered by a 2.7-litre six-cylinder engine with a maximum output of 211 hp. The engine allows the car to accelerate to 100 km/h in 6.4 seconds. The top speed is 245 km/.
